# Rewrite Examples

## Openings

Start with the actual claim, observation, scene, or pressure. Avoid throat-clearing.

Weak:

I've been thinking a lot about how AI is changing product management.

Stronger:

AI product management is starting to split into two jobs: shipping useful systems and explaining why they can be trusted.

## Specificity

Replace vague abstractions with mechanisms.

Weak:

Trust is important for AI products.

Stronger:

Users trust AI products when they can see what the system did, correct it, and understand when a human is still accountable.

## Transitions

Use direct continuation instead of dramatic pivots.

Weak:

But here's the thing.

Stronger:

That creates a product problem.

## Endings

End with a useful final thought, not a motivational summary.

Weak:

The future belongs to teams that embrace AI responsibly.

Stronger:

The trust work has to be designed into the product before the model makes its first mistake in front of a customer.

## Civic: Particular To Universal

Weak:

Trust matters in AI because people need to believe systems are safe.

Stronger:

A user does not lose trust in an AI product when it says "I don't know." They lose trust when it sounds certain and leaves them to discover the mistake alone.

## Civic: Earned Hope

Weak:

AI can help teams work better and build a better future.

Stronger:

AI will make some teams faster and some systems harder to understand. The product work is to make the speed visible, governable, and accountable enough that people can still exercise judgment.

## Civic: Values Through Obligations

Weak:

We need responsible, transparent, human-centered AI.

Stronger:

A responsible AI product tells the user when it is guessing, shows the evidence behind consequential outputs, and gives someone accountable the power to correct it.

## Rhetoric: Symmetry

Weak symmetry:

AI will not replace product managers. It will empower them.

Stronger symmetry:

AI will take away some PM work and expose the rest. The coordination layer gets thinner. The judgment layer gets harder to fake.

## Rhetoric: Antithesis

Good:

The product can make the user faster, or it can make the user less accountable. Great AI design refuses to confuse the two.

Bad:

The future is human plus machine.

## Rhetoric: Repetition

Good:

Show the source. Show the uncertainty. Show the moment where a person has to decide.

Bad:

We need better systems. We need better processes. We need better outcomes.

## Rhetoric: Aphorism

Good:

Trust is not a feeling you add at the end. It is a property of the workflow.

Weak:

Trust is the new interface.

## Rhetoric: Cadence

The model will be wrong sometimes. That is not the scandal. The scandal is building a product where the mistake looks like certainty, moves through the workflow, and reaches the customer before anyone knows who was supposed to stop it.

## Humanizing Detail

Instead of:

Users are frustrated by lack of transparency.

Try:

The user sees a polished answer, sends it to a customer, and only later learns that the source never said what the AI claimed.

## Earned Uplift

Weak:

The future belongs to teams who embrace AI with courage and care.

Stronger:

The durable AI products will be the ones that let people move faster without asking them to surrender their judgment.
